What I do

Star Formation History of the Universe. One of my main research interests is to constrain the star formation rate density throughout the cosmic time. At high redshift (z>4), obtaining these constraints is more challenging. First of all, because most of the time we have to rely on photometric redshifts. Using the data from VIMOS UltraDeep Survey, I provided constraints on the SFRD from a sample of galaxies with secure spectroscopic redshift and two different SFR tracers: UV luminosity and Lya emission. Another challenge is the lack of information about the dust obscured star formation. The FIR emission at z>4 is shifted to mm wavelength and only using facilities such as ALMA, it is possible to directly measure the dust obscured SFRD. These are the constraints obtained using data from ALPINE. In the future, I am planing to continue working on obtaining robust SFRD constraints in the early Universe using JWST data.
